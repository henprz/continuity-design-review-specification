## Overall Assessment

Provide a concise executive summary (2–4 paragraphs).

Rather than summarizing the individual dimensions, synthesize the review into a coherent interpretation of how the product supports continuity.

This section should explain:

- how the product contributes to the user's broader journey;
- the product's strongest contribution to continuity;
- the most significant limitation observed;
- the overall quality of the review based on available evidence.

The summary should communicate not only **how well** the product supports continuity, but also **how it appears to do so**.

A decision-maker should understand the outcome of the review without reading the remainder of the document.

---

# Continuity Interpretation

Before evaluating individual dimensions, synthesize the product's apparent approach to supporting continuity.

This section should interpret the product rather than judge it.

The goal is to explain the product's apparent continuity philosophy as inferred from the available evidence.

The interpretation should explain what the product appears to optimize for, rather than describing interface elements, features, or implementation decisions.

Two products with equally strong continuity may legitimately produce completely different interpretations if they rely on different assumptions about how users make meaningful progress.

## Apparent Continuity Approach

Describe the primary approach the product appears to use to support continuity.

Focus on what the product appears to optimize for throughout the user's broader journey rather than describing visible features or interface behavior.

The description should emerge naturally from the available evidence and should not rely on predefined categories or reusable patterns.

---

## Principal Trade-off

Every approach to continuity emphasizes certain qualities while accepting limitations in others.

Describe the principal trade-off introduced by the product's apparent approach.

The trade-off should emerge from the available evidence and explain what the product appears to prioritize over alternative approaches.

Avoid selecting from predefined categories or generic trade-offs.

The objective is to explain why the product behaves as it does, not merely to describe observed weaknesses.

---

## Interpretation Confidence

Explain how strongly the available evidence supports this interpretation.

This confidence refers to the interpretation itself rather than to individual findings.

For each recommendation, provide one to three short example features that illustrate how the recommendation could be realized.

Recommendations should strengthen the product's apparent continuity approach while reducing its principal trade-offs whenever possible.

Recommendations should improve the product without replacing the strategy that appears to define its approach to continuity.

# Conclusion

Conclude the review by answering the following questions:

1. What journey does the product appear to support?

2. How does the product appear to achieve continuity throughout that journey?

3. Which context hypotheses most strongly shape that approach?

4. How does the product reshape the user's possibility space?

5. Overall, what is the principal opportunity to strengthen continuity without undermining the product's apparent approach?

The conclusion should synthesize the review rather than repeat previous sections.

Different products with similar continuity quality should naturally produce different conclusions if they rely on different approaches to supporting continuity.
