# Guided Review

> Reference template for conducting a Guided Continuity Design Review.

<!--
Reference Review Template

This template demonstrates one compliant implementation of the
Guided Review mode defined by the Continuity Design Review
Specification (CDRS).

Guided Reviews are intended for situations where additional
context can be collected before the evaluation.

Unlike a Quick Review, the reviewer is encouraged to ask
clarifying questions whenever additional information would
materially improve the quality or confidence of the review.

Not every question must be answered.

Unknown information should remain unknown rather than guessed.
-->

# Additional Questions

<!--
Ask only questions that would materially improve the review.

Do not ask questions simply because information is missing.
-->

### Product

- What problem is the product trying to solve?
- What differentiates it from competitors?

### Users

- What motivates users to use the product?
- What alternatives do they currently use?

### Journey

- What happens before users arrive?
- What happens after they leave?
- What defines a successful journey?

### Context

- What information does the product already know about users?
- What information is intentionally ignored?

### Constraints

- Regulatory constraints
- Technical constraints
- Business constraints
- Operational constraints
