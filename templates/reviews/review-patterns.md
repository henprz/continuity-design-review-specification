# Continuity Design Review Patterns

This document illustrates how observations should be expressed during a Continuity Design Review.

The examples below are illustrative.

---

# Pattern 1

❌ Traditional Product Review

"The ranking algorithm prioritizes popular products."

✅ Continuity Design

"The product constructs the hypothesis that popularity is the strongest available indicator of the user's current intent."

---

# Pattern 2

❌

"The autocomplete suggests queries."

✅

"The autocomplete intervenes before the user fully formulates intent, influencing which futures remain visible."

---

# Pattern 3

❌

"The navigation is confusing."

✅

"The product weakens continuity by making the next meaningful step difficult to discover."

---

# Pattern 4

❌

"Users may become frustrated."

✅

"The product interrupts the user's broader journey by repeatedly requiring reconstruction of context."

---

# Pattern 5

❌

"The recommendations are relevant."

✅

"The product appears to maintain useful hypotheses about the user's evolving context."

---

# Pattern 6

❌

"The onboarding explains the product."

✅

"The onboarding accelerates the user's transition from initial uncertainty toward meaningful progress."

---

# Pattern 7

❌

"The interface has many filters."

✅

"The product expands the user's possibility space by exposing multiple meaningful paths for exploration."

---

# Pattern 8

❌

"The search is inaccurate."

✅

"The product repeatedly reinforces an inadequate hypothesis about the user's context."

---

# Pattern 9

❌

"The feature increases engagement."

✅

"The feature encourages continued participation in the user's broader journey."

---

# Pattern 10

❌

"Improve personalization."

✅

"Strengthen the product's ability to refine context hypotheses as new evidence emerges."

---

# Pattern 11

❌

"Provide better recommendations."

✅

"Increase the diversity of meaningful future paths available to the user."

---

# Pattern 12

❌

"The checkout is simple."

✅

"The checkout preserves continuity by minimizing interruptions at a high-commitment stage of the journey."

---

# Pattern 13

❌

"The interface has too much information."

✅

"The product expands the visible possibility space beyond the user's current capacity to evaluate it."

---

# Pattern 14

❌

"The feature helps users."

✅

"The feature supports meaningful progress toward the user's broader journey rather than only the immediate task."

---

# Pattern 15

❌

"The product remembers previous searches."

✅

"The product preserves continuity by carrying forward evidence gathered earlier in the journey."

---

# General Writing Principles

When writing a Continuity Design Review:

- Describe hypotheses rather than implementations.
- Describe journeys rather than isolated tasks.
- Describe interventions rather than features.
- Describe future possibilities rather than current screens.
- Describe continuity rather than correctness.
- Recommend improvements to context hypotheses rather than technical solutions.
- Keep implementation details outside the scope of the review.
