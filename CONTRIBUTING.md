# Contributing

Thank you for your interest in the Continuity Design Review Specification (CDRS).

CDRS is an open specification intended to evolve through discussion, practical application, and continuous refinement.

Contributions are welcome.

---

## Guiding Principles

Any proposed contribution SHOULD support one or more of the following goals:

- Improve the clarity of the specification.
- Increase consistency across the review process.
- Improve the quality of generated reviews.
- Preserve the conceptual integrity of the Continuity Design framework.
- Maintain implementation independence.

Contributions that introduce unnecessary complexity or significantly alter the philosophy of the specification are unlikely to be accepted.

---

## Before Contributing

Before submitting a proposal, please consider the following questions:

- Does this change improve the specification?
- Does it remain consistent with the Continuity Design framework?
- Can existing implementations adopt the change without unnecessary disruption?
- Is the proposal broadly applicable across different products and industries?

---

## Types of Contributions

Examples of valuable contributions include:

- Editorial improvements
- Clarifications
- Additional examples
- Better terminology
- Corrections
- Discussion of ambiguous cases
- Implementation experience reports

---

## Specification Changes

Changes that modify the behavior of the specification SHOULD include:

- the motivation for the change;
- the expected benefits;
- compatibility considerations;
- potential implementation impacts.

Significant changes SHOULD be discussed before implementation.

---

## Code of Conduct

Contributors are expected to communicate respectfully, constructively, and professionally.

Discussions should focus on improving the specification rather than defending individual opinions.

---

## Governance

The Continuity Design Review Specification (CDRS) is currently governed under the **Benevolent Dictator For Life (BDFL)** model.

The original author serves as the final decision-maker regarding the evolution of the specification.

Community feedback, discussions, issue reports, implementation experiences, and improvement proposals are strongly encouraged. However, acceptance of changes remains at the discretion of the BDFL to preserve the conceptual integrity, consistency, and long-term vision of the Continuity Design framework.

The governance model may evolve in future versions if the project reaches a level of maturity that justifies a broader stewardship model.

---

Thank you for helping improve CDRS.
